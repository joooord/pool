## Core Thesis

AI won't replace hospitality. But operators who know how to use it will outcompete those who don't — on margins, guest experience, and speed of execution.

This isn't a "prompt engineering" guide. It's an ops manual. Every automation in here was stress-tested in real operations.

## Target Segments & Their Primary Pain Points

### Tier 1: Small Independent (Cafes, Takeaways, Pubs)
- No dedicated ops manager — owner does everything
- Reactive scheduling, over-ordering, food waste
- Drowning in delivery platform admin (Uber Eats, Just Eat, Deliveroo)
- Zero time for marketing/social
- Terrible review response rate
- Pain: Time poverty. Every saved hour is money.

### Tier 2: Mid-Market (Restaurants, Boutique Hotels, B&Bs)
- Inconsistent guest communication (pre/post stay)
- Manual booking management and upsell opportunities missed
- Staff onboarding is verbal and tribal
- Inventory managed on paper or basic Excel
- Pain: Revenue leakage. Money being left on the table every day.

### Tier 3: Multi-Venue / Resort / Heritage Experiences
- Coordination across multiple departments/sites
- Centralised procurement vs. local flexibility
- VIP and repeat guest recognition gaps
- Pre-arrival comms and upsell sequences weak
- Pain: Scale without chaos. Systems that hold as you grow.

### Tier 4: Transport Hospitality (Heritage Trains, Cruise Lines, Ferries, Airline Lounges)
- Highly fixed capacity = yield management critical
- Captive audience = upsell window is limited and precious
- Dietary requirement management at scale
- Pre-departure communication and anticipation
- Onboard service coordination and real-time adjustments
- Pain: Maximise revenue and experience per passenger per journey.
